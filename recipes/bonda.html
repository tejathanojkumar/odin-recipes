Bonda

Description

Bonda is a popular South Indian snack made from spiced mashed potatoes, dipped in gram flour batter, and deep-fried until golden and crispy. It is often enjoyed with coconut chutney or sambar and is a favorite tea-time treat.

This delicious snack is crunchy on the outside and soft on the inside, making it a perfect comfort food for rainy days or festive occasions.

Ingredients

  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Heat a little oil in a pan, add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add onions and green chilies. Saut√© until onions turn translucent.
  2. Add turmeric powder and mashed potatoes. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes. Add salt and coriander leaves. Let the mixture cool, then shape into small balls.
  3. In a bowl, mix gram flour, baking soda, and salt. Add water gradually to make a thick, smooth batter.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Dip each potato ball into the batter, coat well, and gently drop into the hot oil.
  5. Fry the bondas until golden brown and crisp. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.
